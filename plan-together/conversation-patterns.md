# Conversation Patterns

## Explore → Research → Compare → Converge

1. Explore the user's goal and uncertainty.
2. Research docs, examples, and conventions when they matter.
3. Compare a small number of meaningful options.
4. Converge on a recommendation or next step.

## Convention-gathering

- Check official docs first.
- Inspect examples and prior art.
- Use web search to identify recurring patterns.
- Separate strong convention from flexible taste.

## Teach through the decision

- Explain concepts when they become relevant.
- Prefer mental models over jargon dumps.
- Show why a trade-off matters before recommending a path.
- Say what is standard, what is optional, and what is personal preference.

## Keep momentum

- Ask one narrowing question at a time.
- Summarise before switching modes.
- Avoid wandering once the shape of the problem is clear.
- Do not keep researching after the answer is already obvious.
